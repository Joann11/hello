EXPLAINATION:

Each keyword is asigned to a score of 1 to 4 where 1 is the least and 4 is the most related to the sentimental of a keyword

EXAMPLE:

POSITIVE keyword indicated in green
NEGATIVE keyword indicated in yellow
NEGATION keyword indicated in red
  • POSITIVE: HAPPY:4 means keyword EXCITED has a score of 4 which is the most related to a positive sentiment
  • POSITIVE: FINE:1 means keyword FINE has a score of 1 which is the most related to a positive sentiment
  • NEGATIVE: DEPRESSED:4 means keyword DEPRESSED has a score of 4 which is the most related to a negative sentiment
  • NEGATIVE: BORED:1 means keyword BORED has a score of 1 which is the least related to a negative sentiment
  • TOTAL SCORE > 0 is a positive
  • TOTAL SCORE < 0 is a negative
  • TOTAL SCORE = 0 is a neutral

    • If a sentence is negated by NOT then the score will be affected as such:

  • Negated Positive keyword such as NOT happy will increase the Negative Score
  • Negated Negative keyword such as NOT sad will decrease the Positive score; However, Not Sad does not mean Happy(Positive), so positive score will not be increased
  • The Score is determined by the score of the Negated Keyword such as NOT happy will be -4 if HAPPY:4
